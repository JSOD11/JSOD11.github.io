About Me

In high school, I investigated chemical engineering techniques at Phoenix Diagnostics and was a physics research assistant at Boston College, where I built a new processor for biosening out of a metal box. I've been a machine learning research assistant at Brown and at UChicago, where I worked on transfer learning in RL and applying ML to HCI. I've worked at a wide range of startups in a wide range of roles. I'm currently the CEO of Campus Insights within Harvard Student Agencies, the largest student-run business in the world.

I really like building things, and good stories.
